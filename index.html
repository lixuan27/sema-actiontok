Why this, why now

Robot data is scattered across bodies. Where does the shared language live?

What we build

An action tokenizer that is the alignment layer, not a codec: one vocabulary, trained jointly on mixed-robot data.

Why it matters

A shared action language lets one policy pre-train across robots and reach a new body with a tiny adapter.

What is new

Adaptive length together with a cross-body vocabulary: the corner nobody occupies.

Our position: build on ordered tokenization, then add a learned stop and a vocabulary that outlives the robot it trained on.
