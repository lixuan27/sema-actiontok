Problem

Every robot invents its own action space.

Different dimensions, rates, frames. So every robot gets its own tokenizer, its own vocabulary, its own policy — and nothing transfers.

Framing

A better compressor is not a contribution.

The tokenizer has to enter policy pre-training, carry mixed-robot data, and still beat strong continuous controllers on latency and success.

Latency is a column, never a footnote: success is always reported beside tokens-per-chunk and decode time.

What we found first

We built the variable-length coder. Its adaptivity is a mirage.

Full scale, three seeds. It reconstructs on target — and then almost never stops early, isn't ordered, and mostly decodes out of range. This is why the method exists.
